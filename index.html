The words that make writing sound like AI

Large language models reach for a small, recognizable set of words far more often than most people do. The words ChatGPT overuses include inflated verbs like "delve," "leverage," "underscore," and "elevate"; filler phrasing like "it's important to note" and "in today's fast-paced world"; and vague intensifiers like "incredibly" and "truly." Paste any paragraph into Tellsign above and every one it finds lights up in place, with a note on why it stands out.

Tellsign is not an AI detector. It never claims a paragraph was written by a machine. It's a transparent style checker: the whole list of tells and the scoring math ship in your browser and are readable in the source, so you can see exactly what tripped a highlight, decide for yourself whether it's fair, and edit accordingly. Nothing you paste leaves the page.

Questions people ask

What words does ChatGPT overuse?

The most over-represented are inflated verbs ("delve," "boast," "leverage," "underscore," "foster"), uplift words ("elevate," "unleash," "seamless," "robust"), throat-clearing openers ("it's important to note," "in today's fast-paced world"), and rule-of-three padding. Tellsign catches 80+ of them across six categories and shows each match in context.

Does this sound like AI writing?

Tellsign won't answer that with a yes or no, because no honest tool can. What it does is surface the specific tells in your text so you can judge for yourself. A paragraph thick with flagged words reads as machine-written to a lot of readers; one with none reads as plainer and more human. The meter is a tally of those tells, not a probability of authorship.

What are the AI buzzwords to avoid?

The usual buzzword list includes "leverage," "seamless," "robust," "elevate," "unlock," "empower," "harness," "cutting-edge," and "game-changing." They aren't wrong on their own, but stacked together they read as generated marketing copy. Tellsign flags them so you can swap in something specific.

What are the common ChatGPT tells?

Beyond single words, the ChatGPT tells list includes the rule-of-three cadence ("fast, simple, and reliable"), hedging ("it's worth noting," "generally speaking"), transition crutches ("furthermore," "moreover," "in conclusion"), and disclaimer framing ("as an AI," "it's important to remember"). Tellsign groups its corpus into exactly these categories.

Is Tellsign an AI detector?

No. Detectors output a hidden-model probability you can't inspect or contest. Tellsign shows its work: every highlight names a documented pattern and the reason it's flagged, and it's meant for editing your own writing, not for grading or hiring decisions.
