01

The problem with "87% AI"

Most detectors output a probability with no explanation. A number like that gives you nothing to act on. You can't tell if it's right, can't tell what tripped it, and can't push back on a specific claim. Worse, these tools are frequently wrong in ways that matter, and because the reasoning is hidden, nobody can check their work.

02

What a "tell" actually is

GPT-era prose has a recognizable style: words like "delve," phrases like "it's important to note," the rule-of-three cadence, throat-clearing hedges. That's real and observable, but it isn't proof of authorship. Tellsign is a curated, inspectable corpus of those patterns, matched with plain regex, in your browser. No model call, no network request after the page loads.

03

Who it's for, and who it isn't

Writers, editors, and anyone curious about their own prose who wants a concrete, inspectable style check instead of a verdict. It is not built for academic-integrity enforcement or hiring screens. A curated word list can't support that kind of high-stakes decision, and Tellsign won't pretend otherwise.
